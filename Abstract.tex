	This experiment uses DDS technology to design a direct digital frequency synthesizer based on the Xilinx EGO1 Artix-7 FPGA hardware platform, and after designing, simulating, and debugging on the Xilinx Vivado software platform, it can output four waveforms: sine, square, triangle, and ramp, and can be observed on an oscilloscope through the D/A converter module. The waveforms can be observed on the oscilloscope through the D/A converter module. The synthesizer can also control the frequency of the generated waveform by using a switch as a frequency control word.
	
	In addition, a digital tube display module and a frequency measurement module are added to the direct digital frequency synthesizer. The frequency measurement module can measure the frequency of the waveform generated by the generator, and the display module can display the corresponding theoretical frequency and the measured frequency on the seven-segment digital tube to check the error against each other.
	
	Based on the above functions, the generator also adds digital display, Bluetooth/serial uart transmission of digital strings, and also adds AM modulation function to the signal, which allows AM modulation of sine waves for output.
